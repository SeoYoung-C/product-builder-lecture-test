Menu Library

This library groups 100+ dinner options by cuisine and mood so you can decide faster. Each card includes practical context and a rough budget range.

Cuisine Categories Explained

Korean (Hansik)

From warming soups like gukbap and jjigae to hearty rice dishes like bibimbap and bulgogi — Korean cuisine offers the widest range of options for every budget. Soup-based dishes under $8 provide excellent fullness, making this category ideal for budget-conscious diners.

Chinese (Jungsik)

Korean-style Chinese dishes like jajangmyeon (black bean noodles), jjamppong (spicy seafood noodle soup), and tangsuyuk (sweet-and-sour pork). Known for bold flavors and quick preparation. Great for rainy days and easy to share between two or more people.

Japanese (Ilsik)

Ramen, udon, donkatsu (tonkatsu), sushi, and gyudon (beef bowl). Clean single-dish formats make Japanese food perfect for solo dining. Noodle dishes are served quickly, though sushi and premium options can be pricier.

Western (Yangsik)

Pasta, steak, pizza, risotto, and burgers. This category shines for weekend reward meals and date nights. Dishes that are hard to replicate at home tend to deliver the highest dining-out satisfaction.

Street Food (Bunsik)

Tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), kimbap (seaweed rice rolls), sundae (blood sausage), ramyeon, and fried items. The most budget-friendly category with fun mix-and-match ordering. Perfect for sharing with friends.

Fast Food

Fried chicken, burgers, and pizza chain options. High delivery accessibility with standardized menus that reduce decision stress. Combo meals offer the best value-to-satisfaction ratio in this category.

Details are for general meal planning; pricing and availability can vary by restaurant and region.
